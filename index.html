About

IEEEE is an institute dedicated to the honest documentation of engineering experiments that don’t go as planned—and the essays that make sense of them.

We believe that the most instructive research often comes from the margins: failed replications, unexpected bugs, and “embarrassing” results that traditional venues seldom publish. Our mission is to give these outcomes a home and to turn them into reusable knowledge.

Whether you’re reading our essays or submitting your own embarrassing experiment, you’re part of a community that values transparency and learning over polish.

02

Research Areas

  • [E]

    Embarrassing Experiments

    Rigorous documentation of experiments that failed, backfired, or yielded “wrong” but informative results. Replication packages and post-mortems included.

  • [S]

    Essays & Reflections

    Long-form essays on engineering practice, research culture, and the value of publishing negative or messy results. No paywall, no polish requirement.

  • [O]

    Open Artifacts

    Code, datasets, and lab notebooks from our experiments. Everything we can share, we do—including the parts we’re not proud of.
